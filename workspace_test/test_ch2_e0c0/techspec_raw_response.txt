# Technical Specification: ECG Physics Chapter 2 — From Electrodes to Leads

This specification defines the architectural and creative requirements for the second chapter of the "ECG Physics" series. The document must transition from the microscopic dipole concepts of Chapter 1 to a macroscopic, multi-vector observation system.

## 1. Content Guidelines
The Writer must adopt a **first-principles reasoning style**, moving from physical hardware to mathematical abstraction.
- **Conceptual Framework**: Explicitly distinguish between the "Hardware" (Electrodes as physical transducers) and "Software" (Leads as mathematical vectors).
- **Mathematical Rigor**: Use **dot product notation** ($\vec{V}_{lead} = \vec{D} \cdot \vec{L}$) to explain why the amplitude of a QRS complex is the projection of the cardiac vector onto the lead axis. 
- **The "Camera" Analogy**: Maintain the pedagogical thread that 12 leads are 12 different camera angles observing the same 3D event.
- **Evidence & Rigor**: Citations should reflect standard electrophysiology (e.g., Einthoven, Wilson) and clinical correlations aligned with USMLE Step 1 cardiovascular physiology.

## 2. Visual Design System
The design must mirror a high-end medical journal with a modern, interactive interface.
- **Typography**: 
  - **Headers**: *Noto Serif SC* (Weight 700) for a prestigious, academic feel.
  - **Body**: *Inter* or *system-sans* for high legibility.
  - **Math/Code**: *Roboto Mono* for variables and formulas.
- **Color Palette**: 
  - **Medical Blue** (`#0ea5e9`): Used for "Positive" leads and terminal points.
  - **Heart Red** (`#e11d48`): Used for the cardiac depolarization vector.
  - **Neutral Slate** (`#f8fafc`): Background for formula blocks and SVG containers.
- **Component Styles**:
  - **Formula Blocks**: Centered, slate-50 background, subtle 1px border.
  - **Clinical Correlation Cards**: Border-left (4px) in `heart-red` to denote high-yield board info.

## 3. Interactive Elements
The Transformer and Designer must collaborate on **Inline SVG animations** that visualize abstract physics:
- **Vector Projection Demo**: An interactive SVG where a user can rotate a "Cardiac Vector" (red arrow). As it rotates, the projected shadow on "Lead II" (blue axis) should grow/shrink, simultaneously drawing a corresponding ECG wave (P-QRS-T) to show the relationship between angle and amplitude.
- **The Hexaxial Toggle**: A 12-lead grid where clicking a lead (e.g., aVF) highlights its position on the Hexaxial/Einthoven coordinate system.
- **CSS Effects**: Use **Glassmorphism** for floating labels on SVG diagrams. Implement `transition-all` on lead cards so they scale slightly and change border color when the user explores anatomical groupings (Lateral vs. Inferior).

## 4. Accessibility & Performance
- **Accessibility**: All SVG elements must include `<title>` and `<desc>` tags. Use high-contrast ratios (4.5:1) for text against backgrounds. Ensure the 12-lead grouping interactions are keyboard-navigable.
- **Performance**: 
  - MathJax should be configured for `chtml` output for faster rendering.
  - SVGs must be optimized (minified) and use `viewBox` for perfect scaling across mobile and desktop.
  - Avoid heavy external libraries; rely on Tailwind CSS for 90% of styling.
